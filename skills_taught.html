Skills taught in Foundation Year

The transition from a student’s previous level of qualification to university can be a very hard hurdle for students to overcome as it involves adapting to a new environment, learning new skills, making new friends, amongst others.

Foundation year, a program offered by many universities, including The University of Manchester, serves as the bridge between a student’s previous level qualification and their degree. It serves as a cushion to consolidate some academic concepts so students are more 'ready' for their course. This can also relieve some of the stresses associated with the transition.

The experience one acquires from the foundation year is one that cannot be understimated. Since the foundation year is the bridge the gap between A level and the university, skills such as study skills, working under pressure, adaptation to a new environment are aquired.

Approaches to teaching and learning at the University can be entirely different from A levels. There are more asynchronous sessions compared to the synchronous sections. One must therefore learn to study effectively on their own.
